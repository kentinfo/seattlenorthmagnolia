Background

Understanding the OneSeattlePlan and its impact on North Magnolia

Background Summary

The OneSeattlePlan proposes to upzone the North Magnolia area by creating a North Magnolia Neighborhood Center. This represents a drastic increase in density that is not supported by the unique infrastructure of Magnolia, including geographical factors (peninsula with limited transit) and it puts over 400 residents at risk for displacement by developers.

The current proposal would allow buildings up to 75 feet tall (approximately 7 stories) in an area that is currently primarily single-family homes and low-rise apartments. This dramatic change would fundamentally alter the character of the neighborhood without adequate planning for infrastructure improvements, transit access, or anti-displacement measures.

Our community is not opposed to thoughtful density increases, but we believe any plan must account for the unique geographical constraints of Magnolia as a peninsula with limited access points, consider the current lack of frequent transit service, and protect current residents from displacement.
